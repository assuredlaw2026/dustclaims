Project Background

About the CC-215
Bruce Woodbury Project

The Bruce Woodbury Beltway (CC-215) is the Clark County portion of the 215 freeway encircling the Las Vegas Valley. Las Vegas Paving Corp. holds DCOP 59280 for the "Bruce Woodbury 2025" project, covering 51.10 acres of active construction along the CC-215 corridor near Green Valley Parkway and Stephanie Street in Henderson.

Why This Site Is Under Review

Large-scale grading and earthmoving of the kind underway along the CC-215 corridor can generate significant fugitive dust. With residential and hotel properties immediately adjacent to the work area on both sides of the beltway, dust control is required at all times. Assured Law is monitoring this project and collecting observations from nearby residents.

Applicable Requirements

What the Law Requires
at This Site

At 51.10 acres, this project exceeds the threshold triggering mandatory Dust Control Monitor requirements under Clark County Air Quality Regulations. The following standards apply to every day of active construction.

51+

Dust Control Monitor Mandatory

Any construction project with 50 acres or more of disturbed soil at any given time is required to have a certified Dust Control Monitor present and devoted specifically to dust prevention during all construction activities. This project exceeds that threshold.

CC AQR Section 94.8(a)(1)
24/7

Continuous Dust Control Required

Control measures must be implemented 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. All construction activities must immediately cease when fugitive dust exceeds permitted opacity standards, regardless of weather conditions or project schedule pressures.

CC AQR Section 94.13(b)
100ft

No Emissions Across Property Lines

No visible emissions of particulate matter may extend more than 100 feet or cross a property line. With residential and hotel properties immediately adjacent to this corridor, that standard applies to every pass of every grading machine on this project.

CC AQR Section 94.14(a)(3),(4)
