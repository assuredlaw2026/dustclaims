Why This Site Matters

252 Acres Adjacent
to Residential Neighborhoods

The former Badlands Golf Course represents one of the largest active development sites in the Las Vegas Valley. At 252 acres, this project is more than five times larger than the minimum acreage requiring a Dust Control Operating Permit, and large enough to require mandatory on-site Dust Control Monitoring under Clark County Air Quality Regulations.

252

Acres of Disturbed Soil

Projects disturbing 0.25 acres or more are required to obtain a Dust Control Operating Permit. This site is 1,010 times that threshold. The scale of potential dust impact on neighboring residential properties is significant.

CC AQR Section 94.4.1(b)(1)
DCM

Dust Control Monitor Required

Any construction project with 50 acres or more of disturbed soil at any given time is required to have a certified Dust Control Monitor present during all construction activities. This site triggers that requirement many times over.

CC AQR Section 94.8(a)(1)
100ft

No Dust Across Property Lines

Regardless of permit status, no person engaged in construction activities may allow visible emissions of particulate matter to cross a property line or extend more than 100 feet. Neighboring homeowners have enforceable rights if this occurs.

CC AQR Section 94.14(a)(3),(4)

If You Live Nearby

What to Watch For

If you own a home adjacent to or near the Badlands site, here is what constitutes a reportable violation under Clark County Air Quality Regulations and Henderson Municipal Code. Document anything you observe and contact Assured Law.

Visible Dust Clouds

Any visible dust plume extending beyond the site boundary or crossing your property line is a potential violation. Document with video or photos, showing the source and the plume reaching your property.

After-Hours Construction

Construction activity, equipment operation, or material deliveries outside of permitted hours is a violation. Note the exact time and take video if safe to do so.

Equipment on Public Streets

Heavy construction equipment operating on public residential streets without visible traffic control measures or flaggers may be unauthorized. Photograph the equipment type and street location.

No Water Truck at Start of Day

Clark County regulations require dust control measures to be in place before construction begins each day. Grading, earthmoving, or excavation starting without water application is a potential violation.

Trackout on Public Roads

Mud, dirt, or construction debris tracked from the site onto Rainbow Blvd, Charleston, Rampart, or any adjacent street is a violation. Photograph the deposit and its origin point.

Pool, HVAC, or Surface Contamination

Fine sediment in your pool, on your AC unit and filters, or coating exterior surfaces may be attributable to nearby construction activity. Document with dated photographs before cleaning.

How to Document Observations

Do This

  • Enable location and timestamp on your phone camera before shooting
  • Record video rather than photos when dust is actively moving
  • Write down date, time, wind direction, and what you observed
  • Save receipts for any pool cleaning, filter replacement, or repairs

Send Us

  • Your property address and its relation to the site
  • Dates and descriptions of what you observed
  • Any photos or video you have captured
  • Any prior complaints you filed with any agency and their responses
Your Rights as a Neighbor

What the Law
Protects

Whether or not any agency acts, you have private enforcement rights under Nevada law and Clark County Code. A permit does not give a developer the right to damage your property or your health.

Property Rights
Dust crossing your property line without authorization is a trespass and a nuisance under Nevada common law, regardless of whether a DCOP is in place.
Health Damages
Health impacts arising from airborne particulate matter attributable to construction activity on the site are compensable damages in a private action.
Property Damage
Costs of cleaning pools, replacing HVAC filters, repairing exterior surfaces, and any other repairs necessitated by construction dust are recoverable.
Loss of Use
Inability to use outdoor spaces, pools, or your home as intended due to dust and construction activity constitutes loss of use damages.
Injunctive Relief
Courts can order construction to cease or comply with applicable standards within days of filing. Government inaction does not eliminate this option.
