Yakubik, et al.
v. Pulte Homes

PN II, Inc. and Western States Contracting, Inc.

On July 15, 2026, the Eighth Judicial District Court granted a Temporary Restraining Order against Pulte Homes and its general contractor, ordering them to cease fugitive dust violations, after-hours construction, and unauthorized use of heavy equipment on public residential streets adjacent to the Lake Moor development in Henderson, Nevada.

Court Order

What the Order Requires

Three mandatory restraints effective immediately and through the pendency of any permanent injunction.

1

Dust Control Compliance

Defendants must implement Best Available Control Measures, cease all construction when emissions exceed permitted limits, and not cause or allow visible emissions of particulate matter to extend more than 100 feet or cross a property line.

CC AQR Sections 94.13(c), 94.14(a)(3), 94.14(a)(4)
2

Authorized Hours Only

No construction activity, equipment operation, or material delivery outside the hours authorized by applicable permits and the Henderson Municipal Code.

Henderson Municipal Code, Permit Conditions
3

No Heavy Equipment on Public Streets

Wheel loaders, excavators, and articulated haul trucks may not be operated, staged, or hauled on adjacent public streets except as authorized by applicable permits and approved traffic control plans.

State Law and Permit Conditions

Note

The Order does not prohibit lawful construction activity conducted in compliance with the Order and applicable permits and regulations. Defendants were already required to comply with these standards before the Order was filed.

Enforcement Timeline

Before filing suit, Assured Law followed proper channels. This is the documented record of what was reported and what agencies failed to act on.

March 4, 2026
After-hours work documented at 6:54 p.m. and 7:00 p.m. Heavy equipment operating in active dust cloud at Athens Ave. and Racetrack Rd.
April 8, 2026
Cease and desist letter issued to Western States Contracting, Inc. via certified mail and email. No remediation followed.
May 19, 2026, 2:33 a.m.
Excavator delivered to public residential street. Captured on security camera. No permit or traffic control plan in evidence.
May 21, 2026
Multiple dump truck violations documented. Pool sediment confirmed in neighboring residential pool.
May 27, 2026
Private compliance notice issued to Defendants and forwarded to noticed agencies. No enforcement action followed from any agency.
June 5 to 6, 2026
Additional violations documented. June 6: active dust storm with Henderson Police Department present on-site with no enforcement action taken.
June 8 to 9, 2026
Work began with no water applied. Multiple dump trucks raising dust clouds on residential streets.
July 15, 2026
Temporary Restraining Order Granted. Judge Timothy C. Williams orders immediate compliance. Hearing for permanent injunction set for 7/29/2026.
