Background

What Is the
Three Kids Mine?

A former open-pit manganese mine turned federally recognized contamination site, now being cleaned up while neighboring homeowners bear the cost.

The Three Kids Mine was an open-pit manganese mine discovered in Clark County, Nevada in 1917. During two World Wars, manganese from the mine was used in military weapons and equipment. A mill constructed in 1942 processed ore on-site until all pits were exhausted and operations permanently ceased in 1961.

Left behind were open pits, waste rock, ore yards, a mill site, and tailings ponds loaded with dusts, residues, and processing wastes containing lead, arsenic, manganese, and petroleum, confirmed by a Phase I Environmental Site Assessment in 2007.

Despite these known hazards, parcels from the property were sold off and redeveloped into residential communities. In 2024, land transfer was completed and active cleanup began, but not before years of toxic dust exposure for neighboring homeowners.

In March 2023, the Nevada Division of Environmental Protection (NDEP) hosted a public meeting in Henderson. By November 2023, NDEP issued its Record of Decision for site cleanup. A Perimeter Air Monitoring Plan was put in place as cleanup commenced in May 2024.

Your Rights

Legal Claims Available
to Homeowners

Developers and builders were required by Clark County Air Quality Regulations to prevent toxic dust from reaching your property. Multiple laws were likely violated, and those violations are actionable.

01
CC AQR § 40

Nuisance, Prohibited Air Contaminants

Under Clark County Air Quality Regulations Section 40, no person shall cause or allow discharge of air contaminants that create a nuisance. Toxic dust emissions from construction and remediation activities at the Three Kids Mine site that affected your property constitute a prohibited nuisance condition.

Authority: CC AQR Section 40, Prohibition of Nuisance Conditions
02
CC AQR § 41

Fugitive Dust, Failure to Control

Section 41 requires all persons engaged in demolition, grading, clearing, and construction to take all reasonable precautions to prevent fugitive dust from becoming airborne. Visible dust plumes extending beyond property lines, dust on haul trucks, and trackout onto paved roads near the site are all documented violations triggering liability.

Authority: CC AQR Section 41, Fugitive Dust Prohibitions §§ 41.1.1–41.1.2
03
CC AQR § 90

Open Areas & Vacant Lots, Stabilization Failure

Section 90 mandates that owners and operators of disturbed open areas of 5,000+ sq ft must implement dust control within 30 days of discovering disturbance. Failure to establish a visible soil crust, maintain 20% non-erodible cover, or achieve the required Threshold Friction Velocity constitutes a direct regulatory violation affecting neighboring homeowners.

Authority: CC AQR Section 90, Fugitive Dust from Open Areas and Vacant Lots §§ 90.2.1, 90.2.1.2
04
CC AQR § 94

Construction Dust Permit Violations

Section 94 requires a Dust Control Operating Permit (DCOP) for all construction disturbing 0.25+ acres, with mandatory Best Management Practices, 24/7 dust control, certified Dust Control Monitors for 50+ acres of disturbed soil, and immediate work stoppage when dust plumes exceed 100 yards. Violations of any DCOP condition are enforceable violations of Clark County Air Quality Regulations.

Authority: CC AQR Section 94, Permitting and Dust Control for Construction §§ 94.5, 94.8, 94.13, 94.14
05
CC AQR § 8

Individual and Collective Liability

Section 8 of the Clark County Air Quality Regulations provides that all persons owning, operating, or in control of property who cause, permit, or participate in any violation are individually and collectively liable. This means developers, home builders, contractors, and site operators can all be held responsible for dust exposure at your home.

Authority: CC AQR Section 8, Persons Liable for Penalties § 8.1
06
Common Law

Property Damage & Diminished Value

Beyond regulatory violations, homeowners may pursue claims for diminished property value, cost of remediation of affected property, medical monitoring costs, and personal injury arising from exposure to lead, arsenic, and manganese released during construction and redevelopment activities adjacent to the Three Kids Mine site.

Authority: Nevada tort law, nuisance, trespass to land, negligence, strict liability
Who Qualifies

Are You Eligible?

You may have a claim if you owned residential property near the Three Kids Mine site during any period of active construction or remediation.

You may qualify if you:

  • Own or owned a home in Henderson, NV near the Three Kids Mine site (Lake Las Vegas area, Boulder City corridor, or adjacent neighborhoods)
  • Have experienced visible dust, soil contamination, or air quality issues at or near your property
  • Live within proximity to construction or remediation activities connected to the Three Kids Mine development
  • Have experienced health symptoms consistent with lead, arsenic, or manganese exposure
  • Have noticed dust plumes, trackout, or loose soil from nearby construction or development sites
  • Were not adequately informed of contamination risks before purchasing or building on your property
