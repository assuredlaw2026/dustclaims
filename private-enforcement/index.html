Why Agencies Fall Short

The Enforcement Gap

Regulatory agencies have limited staff, slow processes, and competing priorities. By the time an inspector arrives, violations may have ceased. That does not mean your rights have expired.

Inspectors Are Spread Thin

Air quality inspectors cover all of Clark County. Complaint queues are long. Violations documented on video are sometimes still not acted upon.

Notices Are Not Enough

Notices of Noncompliance and Notices of Violation are paper. They do not stop active violations. Court orders do. And courts can issue them within days of filing.

Police Presence is Not Enforcement

In the Pulte Lake Moor matter, police were documented on-site during an active dust storm on June 6, 2026. No enforcement action was taken. That is why private action exists.

What You Can Do

Your Private Rights

Collective Liability
All persons owning, operating, or in control of property who cause, permit, or participate in violations are individually and collectively liable under Clark County Air Quality Regulations Section 8.
Emergency Relief
Courts can issue Temporary Restraining Orders requiring immediate cessation of violations within days of filing, without waiting for a full trial. Assured Law obtained one against Pulte Homes on July 15, 2026.
Private Nuisance
Unreasonable interference with the use and enjoyment of your property is actionable under Nevada common law, independent of any regulatory violation.
Property Damages
Pool contamination, HVAC damage, surface sediment, street damage from unpermitted equipment, and diminished home value are all compensable in a private action.
Demand Letters
Formal cease and desist letters put contractors and developers on notice and create the pre-litigation record courts consider when granting injunctive relief.
